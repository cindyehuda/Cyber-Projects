# SOC Report (System and Organization Controls)

This README provides an overview of SOC reports, their purpose, and
their relevance for organizations seeking to demonstrate security,
availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy
controls.

## üìò What is a SOC Report?

A **SOC (System and Organization Controls) report** is an independent
audit report completed by a certified CPA firm. It evaluates an
organization's internal controls related to data security and
compliance.

SOC reports are commonly required when:

-   Your organization processes or stores customer data\
-   You want to demonstrate security and compliance to clients\
-   You are working with enterprise customers or regulated industries

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## üè∑ Types of SOC Reports

### **SOC 1**

Focuses on internal controls related to financial reporting.\
Used by organizations whose systems impact customers' financial
statements.

### **SOC 2**

Evaluates controls based on the **Trust Services Criteria (TSC)**: -
Security\
- Availability\
- Processing Integrity\
- Confidentiality\
- Privacy

Most relevant for SaaS companies and service providers.

### **SOC 3**

Similar to SOC 2 but designed for **public distribution**.\

## üìù SOC 2 Report Types

### **Type I**

Describes the organization's system and the suitability of controls at a
point in time.

### **Type II**

Covers operating effectiveness of controls **over 6--12 months**.\
Most requested and trusted by customers.

## üß© Why SOC Reports Are Important

-   Builds customer trust\
-   Demonstrates maturity of security practices\
-   Required by many enterprises before onboarding a vendor\
-   Helps reduce risk across the organization\
-   Provides structure for ongoing compliance efforts

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## üìÇ What Is Covered in a SOC 2 Report?

A typical SOC 2 report includes:

1.  **Management Assertion**
2.  **Description of the System**
3.  **Auditor's Opinion**
4.  **Tests of Controls**
5.  **Results of Testing**
6.  **Additional Information (optional)**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## üöÄ Preparing for a SOC Audit

1.  Perform a **gap assessment**\
2.  Implement required controls\
3.  Establish logging & monitoring\
4.  Ensure onboarding/offboarding procedures\
5.  Document policies and processes\
6.  Engage a certified CPA auditor
